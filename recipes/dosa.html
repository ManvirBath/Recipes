Masala Dosa

Dosa is a crispy, crepe-like and very popular street food in India. It is a thin savory crepe made from fermented batter of ground black lentils and rice. They are served hot, often with chutney and sambar and it is a popular Indian cuisine.

    Ingredients:
  • Dosa Batter (Rice 2 cup and 1 cup urad dal)
  • One teaspoon poha and channa dal
  • Salt
  • Boiled potato
  • Mustard seeds
  • 1 or 2 leaves of kadi patta
  • Basic spices (Red chilly powder, coriander powder, anchur powder, pinch of sugar)
  • Green Chilly
  • Coriandar
  • Onion
  • Coconut, cilantro and tomato chutney
  • 16 oz sambar
    Steps:
  1. Soak both rice and urad daal separately in water for three hours in summer or overnight in winters.
  2. Drain off water. Grind in a grinder into fine paste separately.
  3. Mix toegthered to make a homogenous paste.
  4. Add salt based on preference.
  5. Add water if necessary.
  6. Add a gram of flour.
  7. Grease tawa and heat it on middle heat.
  8. With the help of a ladle or a flat small steel bowl, pour the batter on the stove and spread the batter in circular motions till it forms the shape of a dosa and spreads equally.
  9. Dissolve the sambar powder in a bit of water to spread on the dosa evenly.
  10. Adjust flame according to how crisp you want that dosa.
  11. Spread masala on the dosa.
  12. Roll it up as you wish.
  13. Serve it with the sambar and tomato chutney.
  14. Enjoy!
